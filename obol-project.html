Obol

Welcome to the Obol Portfolio, a showcase of the design journey that brought to life the revolutionary E-wallet, empowering users to explore the world of sustainable investments and forge meaningful connections between small merchants and conscious consumers.

Obol's mission was to bridge the gap between consumers and merchants in the financial market while promoting financial literacy among young adults. The challenge was to create a user-centric application that offered a low-risk platform for exploring investments in small businesses and government funds.

As a sole designer and innovator behind Obol, I undertook multiple roles, including researcher, user tester, UI/UX designer, product designer, and prototype crafter. It allowed me to fully immerse myself in the creative process, ensuring a seamless and holistic user experience.

Market research

While the e-wallet market is currently dominated by major players like PayPal, Google Pay, Apple Pay, and Samsung Wallet, we see an opportunity to differentiate ourselves by offering a more tailored experience for our target audience. Smaller competitors such as Venmo, Dwolla, and Zelle also exist, but our unique proposition lies in our focus on promoting financial literacy and cultivating relationships between consumers and small merchants.

To navigate this ever-changing landscape successfully, we will conduct a thorough SWOT analysis to identify our strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats. This assessment will help us position ourselves strategically within the industry and capitalise on future opportunities

Objective

Obol aims to revolutionize the world of financial transactions by providing a sustainable and efficient platform for shopping, money transfers, and more, without the need for physical debit or credit cards or visiting physical banks or stores. The objective is to cater to individuals who prefer online shopping and transactions but seek a secure and user-friendly experience.

With the growing trend of online retail and transactions, consumers are increasingly opting for the convenience and cost-effectiveness of e-commerce. The 2020 global pandemic further accelerated this shift, as people turned to online shopping to avoid physical contact and potential exposure. Obol addresses the challenges users face in maintaining multiple cards for online transactions and ensures security and efficiency while providing a responsive web app accessible on various devices.

User Persona

Our target audience will be young adult users from 18-35. Users that are approaching the financial market for the first time, want to invest in their future or they simply prefer to promote and shop in smaller business and brands rather than bigger companies. We will focus too in the age range between 35-45 in terms of steady and secure investments, as in government bonds. They also aim towards financial education as they look to invest for the future. We will narrow our user profile to the follow characteristics:

  • Age: 18 - 45 years
  • Gender: no preference
  • Location: mostly everywhere, we will focus on those markets with high amount of digital payment costumers.
  • Education: no specification but we will focus on middle to high education levels
  • Our ideal merchant will be: small business local and sustainability business artisan unique products or self made

Ideation

At Obol, our design criteria go beyond creating just another e-wallet or financial app. Our vision is to be a sustainable financial ecosystem that empowers users to make conscious and responsible financial decisions. We strive to support sustainable projects and promote fair trade in online shopping, ensuring that users' transactions have a positive impact on the environment and society.

To achieve this, Obol offers more than just payment functionality. We are committed to providing budgeting features, gamification and rewards or loyalty programmes that encourage users to make ethical and environmentally friendly choices when shopping online. Our user-friendly interface simplifies complex financial processes and makes it easy for users to understand economic and financial topics. By empowering users with financial knowledge and supporting low-risk investments, we aim to pave the way for a financially secure future for our users while promoting responsible financial practices.

At the heart of Obol's design criteria is the desire to create a platform that not only facilitates finance By giving users the tools to support sustainable projects and engage in fair trade online shopping, we believe we can make a meaningful impact on the global financial landscape. Obol is not just an e-wallet; it is a powerful tool for positive change and responsible financial growth.

UI elements

Navigation

Designing intuitive navigation systems that accommodate multiple features and functionalities, especially for a comprehensive platform like Obol, can be challenging. Ensuring easy access to various sections while maintaining a clutter-free and visually appealing interface requires thoughtful planning.

Information Hierarchy

Establishing a clear information hierarchy to prioritise essential content and actions can be difficult. Balancing the prominence of different UI elements to guide users through the platform efficiently is crucial for a seamless user experience.

Visual Consistency

Achieving a cohesive and consistent visual style throughout the wireframes and establishing design guidelines and ensuring adherence to them is essential for a unified user interface.

Form Design

Designing forms that are easy to complete and understand is a common challenge. Organising form fields, providing helpful hints, and incorporating input validation can reduce user errors and improve the form-filling experience.

Button Placement and Labelling

Deciding on the optimal placement of buttons and labels to promote a seamless flow of actions can be challenging. Clarity in button labeling and ensuring they are located where users expect them can enhance usability.

Iconography

Choosing the right icons and designing them consistently to communicate effectively can be challenging. Ensuring that icons are recognizable, meaningful, and align with Obol's brand identity is crucial for a cohesive visual language.

Accesibility

Designing UI elements that cater to users with diverse abilities can be challenging. Considering colour contrast, font size, and screen reader compatibility to ensure inclusivity and accessibility requires special attention. I had to redesign some part to accommodate to accessibility guidelines
